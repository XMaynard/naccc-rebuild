Crude oil compliance...

It's about the documentation

NAFTA crude compliance is not about condensate. It is not a requirement that applies only to blended crude streams. It is about the origin of the crude oil and the documents the importer has that shows where the crude was produced and what materials were used in its production.

As a result, all crude oil imported into the US require documentation in order to claim trade preference. It does not matter if the crude oil was blended with condensate or not. All types of crude oils, whether it is light crude such as MSW or a heavy dilbit or synbit blend, require appropriate documentation including production records to claim trade preference.

NACCC's statements of origin is based on the production records of the good and on its proprietary tracking method that tracks crude oil from its point of transaction back to its production source.

NAFTA documents are available

We are experts in NAFTA documentation for crude oil.

NAFTA documents are available for crude oil produced in Alberta. Under the NAFTA, trade preference claims have to be supported by documents that demonstrate the origin of the good. The most appropriate way of demonstrating origin of crude oil under the NAFTA is to evidence the production and operation of the facility or wells that produced the crude oil. The available documents are based on tracking the crude oil purchased in Alberta back to its production facility. The operations of the facility, which include the list of materials used in the crude oil blend, form the basis of the statement of origin.

Documents are available for crude oil purchased in Alberta. NACCC is able to provide statement of origins for the crude streams produced in Alberta. The statement of origin is based on the actual production records of the producing facility and NACCC calculates IMS for blended crude streams. Documents are available for the following categories of crude oil products: condensate, light crude oils, heavy blended crudes, and synthetic crude oils.
